What Happened This Week — In Plain English

Last week was one of the best weeks in years. Stocks hit all-time highs. The Nasdaq went up 13 days in a row — a record not seen since 1992. Why? Because people hoped the war between the US and Iran was nearly over.

The good news: Stocks are near all-time highs. Earnings season is strong. Most big banks beat expectations. The economy is resilient.
The caution: Oil went back up today (+2.7%) because the Iran ceasefire ends tomorrow and talks are uncertain. When oil goes up, stocks feel pressure. The market is a bit nervous today — which is normal after a big rally.
Tonight's big event: Tesla reports earnings after the US market closes. This is the most-watched event of the week. Tesla is down 20% this year and the market wants to know — is it still a great company, or just a struggling car company?
