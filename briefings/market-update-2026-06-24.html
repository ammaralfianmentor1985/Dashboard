⚠ Peringatan Kritis — Tekanan Berlapis: Rupiah, Transparansi MSCI, & Daya Saing Industri

Rupiah melemah lagi ke ~Rp17.931–17.945/USD pada pembukaan 24 Juni, kembali mendekati ambang psikologis Rp18.000 — dipicu dolar AS yang melonjak ke level tertinggi 14 bulan pasca pergeseran hawkish Fed, ditambah sentimen domestik yang memburuk. MSCI mengumumkan hasil Market Classification Review 2026 dini hari WIB: Indonesia tetap berstatus Emerging Market, namun MSCI secara eksplisit menyatakan kekhawatiran mendalam atas dugaan perdagangan terkoordinasi dan opasitas struktur kepemilikan saham, dengan keputusan final ditunda ke November 2026 — opsi reklasifikasi ke Frontier Market tetap terbuka jika reformasi (termasuk peningkatan free float minimum 15%) dinilai tidak konsisten. Di sisi industri, dua pabrik komponen otomotif Jepang di Pasuruan & Mojokerto dilaporkan berencana memindahkan sebagian lini produksi ke Vietnam dalam 1–2 tahun ke depan, mengancam ~4.000 pekerja — sinyal erosi daya saing manufaktur domestik di tengah ekosistem EV yang masih kalah kompetitif dari Vietnam (Kementerian Perindustrian membantah adanya bukti relokasi). [Investor.id/Mediaindonesia, 24 Jun 2026; CNBC/Bloomberg/Tempo, 23–24 Jun 2026; Kompas Otomotif/Tribunnews, 22–24 Jun 2026]

⚠ Critical Warning — Layered Pressure: the Rupiah, MSCI Transparency, & Industrial Competitiveness

The rupiah weakened again to ~Rp17,931–17,945/USD at the 24 June open, once more approaching the psychological Rp18,000 threshold — driven by a US dollar surge to a 14-month high following the Fed's hawkish pivot, compounded by deteriorating domestic sentiment. MSCI announced the results of its 2026 Market Classification Review in the early hours WIB: Indonesia retains its Emerging Market status, but MSCI explicitly flagged deep concerns over suspected coordinated trading and opacity in shareholding structures, with a final verdict deferred to November 2026 — a reclassification to Frontier Market remains on the table if reforms (including raising minimum free float to 15%) are judged inconsistent. On the industrial front, two Japanese auto-component plants in Pasuruan and Mojokerto are reportedly planning to shift part of their production lines to Vietnam over the next 1–2 years, threatening roughly 4,000 jobs — a signal of eroding domestic manufacturing competitiveness amid an EV ecosystem still trailing Vietnam's (the Ministry of Industry denies finding evidence of relocation). [Investor.id/Mediaindonesia, 24 Jun 2026; CNBC/Bloomberg/Tempo, 23–24 Jun 2026; Kompas Otomotif/Tribunnews, 22–24 Jun 2026]

⚑ Catatan Kewaspadaan — Streak Outflow Memanjang Meski Mengecil, Inflasi Naik Bertahap

Investor asing memperpanjang streak net selling di Bursa Malaysia ke pekan ke-6 berturut-turut, meski besaran outflow mengecil tajam menjadi hanya -RM10,7 juta (pekan hingga 22 Jun) dari -RM2,53 miliar di awal Juni — sektor Healthcare (-RM108,8 juta) dan Telekomunikasi (-RM106 juta) memimpin outflow, sementara Technology justru mencatat inflow +RM128,2 juta. FBM KLCI bergerak mendatar di kisaran 1.700, tertahan oleh ketidakpastian global menjelang perundingan damai AS–Iran. Ringgit tertekan ke ~RM4,14/USD sejalan kekokohan dolar AS yang luas, sementara Bank Negara Malaysia menahan OPR di 2,75% sejak Juli 2025. Inflasi Mei naik ke 2,0% (dari 1,9%) — tertinggi sejak Juli 2024 — dan berisiko bertambah jika rencana pencabutan subsidi RON95 untuk kelompok T20 direalisasikan. Tekanan ini jauh lebih moderat dibanding Indonesia, namun tetap layak dipantau. [BusinessToday, 22 Jun 2026; The Star, 23 Jun & 7 Mei 2026; TradingEconomics, 2026]

⚑ Watch Note — Outflow Streak Extends but Shrinks, Inflation Edges Higher

Foreign investors extended their net-selling streak on Bursa Malaysia to a sixth consecutive week, though the outflow size shrank sharply to just -RM10.7 million (week to 22 Jun) from -RM2.53 billion in early June — Healthcare (-RM108.8 million) and Telecommunications (-RM106 million) led outflows, while Technology posted a +RM128.2 million inflow. The FBM KLCI traded flat near the 1,700 level, held back by global uncertainty ahead of US–Iran peace talks. The ringgit eased to ~RM4.14/USD in line with broad US dollar strength, while Bank Negara Malaysia kept the OPR at 2.75% since July 2025. May inflation rose to 2.0% (from 1.9%) — the highest since July 2024 — with further upside risk if the planned removal of RON95 subsidies for T20 households goes ahead. This pressure is far more moderate than Indonesia's, but still warrants monitoring. [BusinessToday, 22 Jun 2026; The Star, 23 Jun & 7 May 2026; TradingEconomics, 2026]
