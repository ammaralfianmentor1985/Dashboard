Market Overview
What Happened Overnight & This Morning

Global stock markets are opening this week with a quiet sense of relief. After a turbulent month driven by a US-Israel-Iran military conflict that began in late February, investors are seeing faint but real signals that a ceasefire may be getting closer. This is pushing stock prices gently upward and, importantly, pulling the price of oil — the fuel that powers inflation — noticeably lower today.

The S&P 500 — the benchmark for the 500 biggest American companies — is hovering near 7,234, touching levels that would have seemed extraordinary just a year ago. The Nasdaq, home to technology giants, ended last week at a record high of 25,114. Meanwhile, the fear gauge (VIX) at 16.78 tells us that professional traders are relatively calm — not complacent, but not panicking.

The biggest story this week is not what happened over the weekend, but what is coming: a critical week of US employment data, culminating in Friday's all-important Jobs Report. Markets will listen very carefully to every number that arrives.

🔒 Simple Rule to Protect Yourself — The Seatbelt Rule

Every time you open a trade, you must set a Stop Loss. Think of it exactly like a seatbelt. You do not put on a seatbelt because you plan to crash — you put it on because accidents happen. A Stop Loss automatically closes your trade if it moves against you, so you can never lose more than you decided in advance. Every trade in this document includes a Stop Loss. Always use it.

⏸️ Earnings Alert — When In Doubt, Stay Out

Major company results (AMD, Palantir, Walt Disney, Uber) are being announced this week. Stock prices can jump OR fall 10–15% in minutes after an earnings announcement. Starter clients should avoid technology stocks until results are published. Patience is a skill, not a weakness.
