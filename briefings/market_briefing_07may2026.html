WHY RISK MANAGEMENT MATTERS MOST AT ALL-TIME HIGHS
When a market reaches record levels — as the S&P 500 has done this week — the most common mistake is complacency. "It's going up anyway, I don't need a stop." History is unambiguous: the sharpest corrections often begin from record highs, precisely because everyone is already invested and there is no one left to buy. The US-Iran situation remains genuinely dangerous. A single headline confirming military escalation could see oil spike to $130 and stocks drop 5–8% in hours. Your stop losses are not just bureaucracy — at all-time highs, they are your most important tool. Market conditions can change faster than you can pick up the phone.

🇲🇾
Malaysia (MYR)
Oil Surge = Mixed Signal for Ringgit
Malaysia, as a net oil exporter (Petronas revenues), typically benefits from higher oil prices — the Ringgit tends to strengthen when oil rises. However, when oil surges due to war risk rather than genuine demand, the benefit is complicated by broader emerging-market risk aversion. For your Malaysian clients: higher oil is good for Petronas and domestic energy revenues, but the uncertainty is negative for broader portfolio risk. Advise holding gold exposure and watching the MYR/USD pair — if MYR strengthens, it reduces the USD returns on offshore positions. Rebalancing discussions worth having with clients positioned heavily in USD assets.
