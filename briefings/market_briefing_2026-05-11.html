🛡️ The Seatbelt Rule — How Stop Losses Work
A stop loss is your automatic seatbelt. You set it before you enter the trade — and if the market moves against you by a set amount, it closes your position automatically. You don't have to watch the screen all day. Rule of thumb: never risk more than 5–8% of your account on one trade. On a $2,000 account, that means a maximum loss of $100–$160. Set it before you enter. Never move it further away once the trade is open. If you feel tempted to remove your stop loss, that is the exact moment you should keep it in place.
