Section 1 — What Happened
The Story in Plain English

Think of markets like a kitchen: too much heat (inflation) makes everything expensive, burns the budget, and forces the chef (the Fed) to turn down the gas. That's exactly where we are.

✅ The Good News

Corporate America is delivering — Q1 2026 earnings grew a stunning 27.1%, the best since late 2021. Companies are still making money even in a high-cost environment. The Dow Jones held flat today, showing that old-economy, dividend-paying companies (banks, manufacturers, retailers) remain resilient. If you own quality businesses, the story is still good.

⚠️ The Caution

Inflation is the uninvited guest that won't leave. US consumer prices rose 3.8% in April — the highest since May 2023 — driven almost entirely by energy. Petrol prices jumped 28% annually because the Strait of Hormuz (the world's most important oil shipping lane) is effectively blocked due to the US–Iran conflict. Yesterday's factory-price data (PPI) was even hotter at 6% annually, the sharpest since 2022. Tech stocks fell hardest today — Nasdaq dropped 1.34% — as higher inflation means interest rates stay high, which hurts future profits for growth companies.

📅 Today's Biggest Event

US April Retail Sales are released today — this measures how much ordinary Americans are actually spending. If the number is weak, it signals consumers are buckled under inflation. If it's strong, it confirms the economy is still resilient despite the pressure. This single number could move every major market by 0.5–1.5% in either direction. Watch for it around the US market open. Also today: Applied Materials (AMAT) reports earnings — a key barometer for the semiconductor and AI supply chain.

🔒 Simple Rule to Protect Yourself — The Stop Loss Seatbelt

A stop loss is exactly like a car seatbelt. You don't put it on because you plan to crash — you put it on because the road ahead is unpredictable. A stop loss automatically closes your trade if the market moves against you by a set amount, so you can't lose more than you've already decided is acceptable. Every single trade you ever open must have a stop loss attached. No exceptions. A trade without a stop loss is like driving a motorway at 120km/h with no seatbelt — fine until it isn't.

⚠️ Why Risk Management Matters Most at Elevated Levels

The S&P 500 is near all-time highs. When you are near the top of a mountain, every step carries the risk of a fall — not because falls are inevitable, but because the distance to the bottom is greater. At elevated market levels, the asymmetry shifts: the potential downside from a shock (a failed Iran deal, a hotter-than-expected inflation print, a Fed surprise) is larger than the remaining upside. This is not a reason to sell everything. It is a reason to have every position sized correctly, every stop loss in place, and enough cash to act when fear creates opportunity. The investors who survived 2020, 2022, and 2025's oil shock did so not because they predicted the crash — but because they never let one bad day become a catastrophic year.
