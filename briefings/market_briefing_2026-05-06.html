🛡️ Simple Rule to Protect Yourself — The Stop Loss is Your Seatbelt

A stop loss is a pre-set instruction you give your trading platform that says: "If this trade goes wrong and reaches this price, close it automatically." Just like a seatbelt — you don't plan to have an accident, but you always wear it anyway. Never open a trade without setting a stop loss. The levels shown in the tables above are where you set it. Once set, do not move it further away just because you hope the market will turn around. That is like unbuckling your seatbelt halfway through a crash.

Why Risk Management Matters Most Right Now
Markets near all-time highs are like a glass that is completely full — even a small knock can cause a spill. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq are at or near record levels while oil is simultaneously surging, creating a rare and unstable combination. History shows that markets at all-time highs with elevated VIX and geopolitical stress tend to experience sharp corrections of 5–10% with very little warning. This is not a reason to exit all positions — it is a reason to ensure every position has a clearly defined stop loss, that you are not using excessive leverage, and that you have dry powder (cash) available to buy any sharp dips. The risk of missing a further 10% rally is always smaller than the risk of riding a 20% drawdown without a plan.
