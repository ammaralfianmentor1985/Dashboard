🚗 Your Simple Rule to Protect Yourself: The Seatbelt Principle

A stop loss is exactly like a seatbelt. You don't wear a seatbelt because you plan to crash — you wear it just in case. When you set a stop loss on your trade, it automatically exits your position if the market moves too far against you. It limits how much you can lose. You wouldn't drive without a seatbelt, so please don't trade without a stop loss. It's the single most important habit you can build as a new trader — and it's always included in every idea in this briefing.

Indonesia is a net oil importer. When Brent rises above $100, it pressures the government's fuel subsidy bill and widens the current account deficit — both negatives for the rupiah. USD/IDR at 17,370 reflects this stress. Conversations with Indonesian clients should acknowledge that their day-to-day costs (petrol, food delivery, transport) are likely rising. Trading with a USD-denominated account actually provides a natural hedge for IDR-based clients in this environment.

Malaysia occupies the opposite position to Indonesia — it is a net oil and LNG exporter via Petronas. Higher oil prices are a fiscal boon for Kuala Lumpur. Conversations with Malaysian clients: acknowledge the local macro benefit, but note that global volatility can still drag domestic markets lower in the short term.
